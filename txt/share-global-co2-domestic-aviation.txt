The chart presents the percentage shares of global domestic CO2 emissions from domestic air travel for various countries in the year 2018. The United States leads substantially with a 16.9% contribution to global domestic CO2 emissions from air travel. China follows, accounting for 8.6% of the emissions, while India contributes 1.4%. The United Kingdom and New Zealand contribute the least among the listed countries, with the U.K. at 0.2% and New Zealand at a mere 0.1% of the global domestic CO2 emissions from domestic air travel.
