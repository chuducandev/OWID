The chart illustrates the proportion of forest area within legally established protected areas in Georgia from the year 2000 to 2020. In 2000, the proportion stood at 7.91%, showing a slight decrease by 2010 to 7.85%. However, from 2010 to 2020, there has been a clear upward trend. In 2015, the proportion increased to 9.7% and slightly dropped to 9.46% by 2018. Notably, by 2020, the proportion of forest area in protected zones showed a significant increase, reaching 11.19%. The overall trend indicates a positive movement towards conserving more forest areas under protection over the two-decade span.
