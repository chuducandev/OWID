The chart illustrates the percentage of adults who consumed alcohol in the past 12 months across three countries for the year 2016. Lithuania stands out with a significantly high proportion of alcohol consumers, at 79.1%, which is more than triple the percentage of alcohol consumers in Kenya, which has the lowest percentage at 23.9%. Kyrgyzstan lies in between, with 25.9% of its adult population having consumed alcohol in the same timeframe. This stark contrast highlights Lithuania as having a notably higher prevalence of alcohol consumption among adults compared to Kenya and Kyrgyzstan.
