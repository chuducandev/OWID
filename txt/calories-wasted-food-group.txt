The chart indicates the proportion of calories lost or wasted by food group globally in 2009. Roots and tubers experience the highest level of waste, with a staggering 63% of calories lost. Fruits and vegetables also face significant wastage, at 42%. On the lower end, cereals constitute a 26% loss, and fish and seafood are close by with a 24% wastage rate. Meat and milk are more efficiently used, with only 19% and 18% of calories wasted, respectively. The least waste occurs with oilseeds and pulses, where just 10% of calories are lost. These figures underscore the substantial difference in wastage rates between various food groups, highlighting a particularly serious issue with perishable goods like roots and tubers, and fruits and vegetables.
