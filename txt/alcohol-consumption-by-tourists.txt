The chart presents data on the average alcohol consumption by tourists in various countries for the year 2010. Tourist consumption is measured in liters of pure alcohol and reflects amounts ranging from 0.16 to 3.1 liters. Estonia stands out as the country with the highest reported tourist alcohol consumption at 3.1 liters. Austria follows with 2.25 liters. On the lower end, Malaysia shows the least alcohol consumption among tourists with just 0.16 liters, followed closely by Singapore with 0.41 liters. Other countries like Qatar and the United Arab Emirates show moderate consumption levels at 0.76 and 0.9 liters respectively. These figures indicate a considerable variation in tourist drinking habits across different countries, reflecting cultural, legal, and social differences towards alcohol consumption in those nations.
