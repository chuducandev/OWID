The chart presents data on domestic aviation CO2 emissions for select entities in the year 2018. The United States leads significantly with CO2 emissions amounting to approximately 126.1 million tons. The European Union follows at about 66.86 million tons, with China close behind, contributing roughly 64.54 million tons. India, with emissions around 10.78 million tons, also contributes notably, but less than the leading entities. On the lower end, Australia and the United Kingdom reported emissions of approximately 6.65 million tons and 1.51 million tons, respectively. Portugal and the United Arab Emirates have markedly smaller figures, with Portugal emitting around 440,505 tons and the United Arab Emirates with only about 1,327 tons, showcasing the vast differences in domestic aviation CO2 emissions among these regions.
