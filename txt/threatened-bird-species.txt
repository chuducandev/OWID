In the year 2018, Brazil had the highest number of threatened bird species among the listed countries, with 175 species at risk. Following Brazil, Indonesia had a significantly high number of threatened species as well, with 160. Not far behind, China and India also had high counts, with 96 and 93 threatened bird species, respectively. Meanwhile, the United States was just a notch lower with 91 species under threat. Other countries with notable but lower counts included New Zealand with 69, Australia with 52, and Madagascar with 37 species. Cuba had the lowest count among those listed, with 19 threatened bird species. These numbers reflect concerns regarding biodiversity and the varying degrees of threat that bird species face across different regions.
