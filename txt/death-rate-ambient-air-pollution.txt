The chart presents age-standardized mortality rates attributed to ambient air pollution for the year 2019 in four different countries. Turkmenistan exhibits the highest mortality rate due to ambient air pollution among the listed countries, with a rate of 87 deaths per 100,000 population. Nigeria follows with a significant mortality rate of 77 deaths per 100,000. A notable decrease in the mortality rate is seen in Belize, which has a rate of 21 deaths per 100,000. France shows the lowest mortality rate among the four, with 10 deaths per 100,000 population due to ambient air pollution. This data highlights the variance in health impacts from air pollution across different geographic and socioeconomic regions.
