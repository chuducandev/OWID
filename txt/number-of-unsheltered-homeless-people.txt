The chart tracks the escalation of rough sleeping in England from 2010 to 2017, demonstrating a significant increase over the years. Starting at 1,768 individuals in 2010, there is a steady climb each subsequent year. Notably, the rise becomes sharper after 2014. The year 2015 marked an important threshold as the number jumps to 3,569, indicating an increase of over 1,000 people compared to the previous year. The trend of rising homeless figures continues, with 2016 seeing a further hike to 4,134 individuals. The data culminates in 2017 with the total number of people experiencing rough sleeping reaching its peak at 4,751. This figure represents nearly a threefold increase from the count in 2010, and symbolizes a worrying trend in the social issue of homelessness in England during the depicted period.
